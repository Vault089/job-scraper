## Executive Summary

Two distinct job markets exist in parallel: **international schools** (premium pay, credential-heavy, competitive) and **language centers / in-school programmes** (lower barriers, faster hiring, high volume). Your strongest immediate play is the language-center track — you have fresh postings, low competition in regional cities, and interviews flowing within days. The international school track is a longer-cycle investment (3–6 months) that builds toward higher salaries ($3,500–$6,500/mo). Strategy below covers both simultaneously.

## Tailored Approach Per Segment

### Language Centers (Tier 1 targets)
- **Voice:** Confident, casual professional. You have IELTS/secondary expertise — lead with that.
- **CV emphasis:** Lesson planning, classroom management, student outcome data (test scores, pass rates).
- **Cover letter hook:** "I'm currently based in Vietnam and available for immediate interview" — removes timezone friction.
- **Follow-up:** If no response in 5 business days, message the recruiter directly on LinkedIn with a brief note.

### International Schools (Tier 2–3 targets)
- **Voice:** Formal, credentialed professional. Lead with qualifications (degree, teaching license, PGCE if held).
- **CV emphasis:** Curriculum frameworks (IB, IGCSE, Cambridge), professional development, international experience.
- **Cover letter hook:** "I'm targeting a Jan 2027 / Aug 2026 start date" — signals long-term planning, reduces urgency pressure.
- **Supporting docs:** Upload a demo lesson video (10 min) or student work samples portfolio to the application.
- **Follow-up:** International schools often have 4–8 week response cycles. A single follow-up at week 4 is appropriate.
